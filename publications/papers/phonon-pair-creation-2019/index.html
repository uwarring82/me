Phonon pair creation by inflating quantum fluctuations in an ion trap

M. Wittemer, F. Hakelberg, P. Kiefer, J.-P. Schröder, C. Fey, R. Schützhold, U. WarringORCID, T. Schaetz

Physical Review Letters 123, 180502 (2019) · Published 31 October 2019 · DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.123.180502

Read at publisher →

Author summary

Can a single trapped ion simulate the phonon creation that happens when a cosmological horizon recedes? In an expanding universe, vacuum fluctuations of quantum fields are inflated into populations of real particles — the same mechanism, in a different setting, drives the dynamical Casimir effect and Hawking-like radiation. Parametric modulation of a trapped ion’s confinement imprints an analogous inflation on the ion’s motional vacuum: initially empty modes become populated with correlated phonon pairs. We demonstrated this process with a single ²⁵Mg⁺ ion, reconstructing the two-mode squeezed state produced by the drive and extracting the pair correlations that distinguish genuine vacuum inflation from thermal heating. The experiment does not claim to reproduce cosmological physics; it realises a formally identical Bogoliubov transformation in a clean, controllable system, where the underlying assumptions can be interrogated one at a time.

Why this mattered

Cosmological particle creation, the dynamical Casimir effect, and Hawking radiation share a common formal skeleton — a Bogoliubov transformation of vacuum modes. Laboratory analogues of any of them are scarce and typically noisy. A trapped-ion analogue lets us verify the formal structure under conditions where the input state, the drive, and the readout are all under experimental control.
